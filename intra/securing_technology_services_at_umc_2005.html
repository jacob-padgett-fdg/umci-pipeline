Securing Technology Services at UMC



It is important for you to know and follow the process for making sure your technology tools are kept in running order.


First, a reminder of the “Basics”

All UMC technology hardware and software is purchased and installed by our IT staff. As is laid out in our employee handbook, it is against UMC’s policy for anyone to download or install software of any kind (even if you think you have a valid software user agreement); or, add or delete any hardware components from UMC equipment. That includes, but is not limited to: instant messaging services, Internet music services, etc. See your UMC Employee Handbook for additional important rules and guidelines.


“Who do I call if I need help with a printer, copy machine, my PC, etc?”


Between 7:30am and 4:30pm weekdays, your first point of contact is Chris Bondelid. Jeff, and myself will assist with the solution only as required. Please use the following methods to inform Chris that you need assistance:

  1. Use the “IT Request” on the UMC Intranet, which can be found on the UMC intranet homepage at pipeline.umci.com. We think it is self-explanatory from there, but ask if you have questions. Be sure to completely fill in all requested information.

  2. If you cannot get to the intranet but you have email, send Chris an email with the pertinent information.

  3. If email and the intranet are not available, contact Chris at 206-368-6211 or call Mary at extension 200 (206-364-9900 from outside the office)


“What do I do if Chris is not available, none of the above works, and I cannot work because of a technological issue?”


Follow the escalation process below:



If Chris is available, please contact me at extension 212 or 206-910-5062.


If I am not available, you cannot work because of a technological issue, and tomorrow is not an acceptable time to wait for a solution, you may contact Jeff Buck at extension 255 or call Mary at extension 200 (206-364-9900 from outside the office). Jeff’s primary focus is on our custom database systems.


“What if I need new hardware or software?”


Discuss the tools that you would need to do your job effectively and efficiently with your supervisor. If your supervisor concurs, your supervisor will put in the IT request. This request will be evaluated by the IT staff and myself and you will be notified shortly.



“What can I expect as service hours during the day?”


We try to have someone in the office who can and will assist you from 7:30am to 5pm Monday through Friday including someone here during lunch.


Chris’s typical hours are M-F 7:30am to 4:30pm with lunch from noon-1pm

Jeff’s typical hours are M-F 8am-5pm with lunch from 1pm-2pm



“What can I expect when I submit an IT request?”


When you submit an IT request on the intranet, you will be notified when:

  1. Your request has been reviewed and prioritized;

  2. Your request has been rescheduled for any reason;

  3. Your request has been completed.


We would greatly appreciate if you would send a follow-up email to Chris letting him know your issue is resolved to your satisfaction.


Final Escalation:


I am confident that Chris and Jeff will meet your needs. However, if you are not getting what you need to do your job in a timely fashion, come see me. It is an open door policy.




Oh yeah: “What if I just have some technology questions to shoot the breeze with the IT guys about?”


Do not come down to their office or ask as they are walking down the hall. You may send an IT request and request some time during lunch…..(your treat)!
